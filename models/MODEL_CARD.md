# Model card: constrained linear-unmixing autoencoder

## What this model is

`autoencoder.pt` is a small constrained autoencoder whose decoder is the
linear mixing model itself. The encoder (MLP, 200 -> 96 -> 48 -> 3, ReLU)
maps one EELS spectrum to three logits; a softmax turns them into abundances
that are non-negative and sum to one; reconstruction multiplies them by a
3 x 200 endmember matrix kept non-negative through a softplus
parametrization. 24,699 parameters in total. Everything the network
learns is readable as endmember spectra and abundance maps by construction.

## Training data and protocol

Trained this session on exactly one simulated scene: the committed sample
`data/sample/oxide_interface_d1000.npz` (64 x 64 x 200 cube, dose 1000
counts per pixel spectrum, 1.5 channels drift, seed 0), generated by
`eelsunmix.sim`. Full-batch Adam, learning rate 3e-3, 1200 epochs, seed 0,
MSE on dose-normalized counts. Reproduce with:

    eelsunmix train --dose 1000 --epochs 1200 --seed 0 --output models/autoencoder.pt

## Intended use and scope

This artifact exists so `eelsunmix demo` runs instantly and so the hero
figure regenerates from a fixed model. The unmixing autoencoder is
scene-specific by design: like NMF, it is fit to the cube being analyzed,
not trained once and deployed. For any other cube (different scene, dose,
energy axis, or real data), retrain with `ae_unmix` or the CLI; do not
reuse these weights.

## Measured performance (committed sample, this session)

Scored against the sample's exact ground truth:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Mean spectral angle, matched | 7.53 deg |
| Per phase (Ti / Mn / Fe oxide) | 4.83 / 8.09 / 9.66 deg |
| Abundance RMSE (sum-to-one maps) | 0.072 |

Verify these against the committed artifacts at any time with
`eelsunmix demo`.

The wider picture (dose, drift, and overlap sweeps, seed stability,
component-count sensitivity) is in RESULTS.md; the seed spread and the
training-length caveat below are part of the honest reading.

## Known limitations, measured

- Seed sensitivity: the loss surface is non-convex; `results/stability.json`
  quantifies the spread over 10 seeds at dose 1000.
- Training length is a real hyperparameter: reconstruction error keeps
  falling with more epochs while endmember recovery peaks and then degrades
  (`results/ae_epochs.json`, figures/ae_epochs.png). There is no
  ground-truth-free signal that finds the optimum, because fit error keeps
  improving; the shipped 1200 epochs sits near the optimum on this scene
  family, and that choice is disclosed rather than hidden.
- Trained on this simulator's physics only: linear mixing plus Poisson noise
  and smooth energy drift. No plural scattering, no spectrometer PSF, no
  gain variation. Real-data behavior is untested and unclaimed.
